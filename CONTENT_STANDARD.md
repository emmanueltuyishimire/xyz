## KEY STANDARDS AT A GLANCE

### Page Type Minimum Word Counts
- Template A (Calculator/Tool): 1,500 words of educational content (not counting the tool)
- Template B (Educational Guide): 2,000 words
- Template C (Notice Decoder): 1,800 words
- Template D (Hub): 1,200 words + links to sub-pages

### Required Sections by Template

**Template A — Calculator/Tool:**
1. H1 Title: [Tool Name] — [Plain English Benefit Statement]

3. Opening Statement (2–4 sentences)
4. THE TOOL (interactive calculator)

## VOICE RULES (non-negotiable)

1. Calm and reassuring FIRST — before explaining any rule or penalty
2. Respectful — never condescending, never "it's simple"
3. Warm — not clinical or robotic
4. Active voice ONLY — "Social Security will reduce your benefit" not "benefits may be reduced"
5. Max 20 words per sentence
6. Max 60 words / 4 lines per paragraph
7. One idea per paragraph
8. Define every acronym on first use
9. Use specific numbers (not "significantly reduce" — say "by exactly 30%")
10. Say "you" — never "the claimant" or "the beneficiary"
11. End every section with a clear next step

## SEO RULES

- Meta description: 145–158 characters, include keyword, end with subtle CTA
- Primary keyword: in H1, first 100 words, one H2, naturally throughout

- Min 3 internal links per page with descriptive anchor text (never "click here")

## READABILITY TARGETS

- Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: 6–8
- Preferred simple words: get / find out / look at / tell / use / start / end / check / ask
- Spell out numbers 1–10 in body text; use numerals for 11+, dollars, percentages
- Write "$185.00" not "$185" or "185 dollars"
- Write "30% — meaning three dollars out of every ten" for percentages

## NEVER USE

- "It is simple" / "It is easy" / "Simply do X"
- "Obviously" / "Clearly" / "As you know"
- "The claimant" / "the beneficiary" / "the user"
- "Guaranteed" / "You will definitely receive"
- Passive voice
- Paragraphs over 60 words
- Sentences over 20 words
- Fear-based language without immediate reassurance + solution
- Jargon without immediate definition
- "Click here" as anchor text
- Government logos, seals, or emblems
- Any claim of government affiliation

## SECTION 11: DO NOT WRITE LIKE AN AI — THIS IS NON-NEGOTIABLE

### 11A: Typography and Punctuation Patterns to Never Use
- **The em dash in the middle of sentences — like this — is an AI signature. Never use it.** Use short sentences and periods instead.
- **Never turn everything into bullet points.** Use natural flowing sentences. Bullets are only for lists, steps, and options.
- **Never bold random phrases throughout the body text.** Only bold warning labels, form/publication names, and key dollar amounts in results.
- **Do not use multiple punctuation types to pad sentences.** No semicolons connecting clauses, no unnecessary parentheses or ellipses.
- **Do not use the hyphen ( - ) where a period or comma belongs.**

### 11B: AI Phrases that Must Never Appear on Any Page
- **Opening sentence traps:** "When it comes to...", "Navigating the complex world of...", "In today's world/landscape...", "[Topic] affects millions of Americans...", "As a senior, you have worked hard..."
- **Filler transitions:** "Furthermore,", "Moreover,", "Additionally,", "In conclusion,", "To summarize,", "In summary,", "In essence,", "Needless to say...", "At the end of the day...", "As mentioned above/earlier..."
- **Hollow hedging:** "It is important to note that...", "It is worth mentioning that...", "It is crucial to understand that...", "Please note that...", "Keep in mind that..."
- **AI enthusiasm:** "Absolutely!", "Certainly!", "Of course!", "Great question!", "Let's dive/delve/explore in...", "Without further ado..."
- **Inflated vocabulary:** "utilize" (use "use"), "facilitate" (use "help"), "ascertain" (use "find out"), "endeavor" (use "try"), "leverage" (use "use"), "delve into" (use "look at"), "shed light on" (use "explain"), "comprehensive" (use "complete"), "navigate" (be specific).
- **Fake empathy:** "We understand how confusing this can be.", "You deserve clear answers.", "We know this is a stressful situation." Open with specific, contextual empathy instead.

### 11C: Structural AI Patterns to Avoid
- **Avoid perfect symmetry:** Real writing has structural variation. Do not make every section have the exact same number of paragraphs or bullet points.
- **Do not restate the title in the first sentence.**
- **Do not end with a generic summary/conclusion.** End with next steps or clear actions.
- **Do not use "First... Second... Third... Finally..." as a structural skeleton.**
- **Do not fake Q&As with short, one-sentence answers.** Real answers are 4 to 8 sentences, providing context, examples, and actions.
